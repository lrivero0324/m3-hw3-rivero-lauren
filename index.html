Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart: A Classical Genius

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart (1756-1791)

About Mozart

Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was a prolific and influential composer of the Classical era. Born in Salzburg, Austria, he showed prodigious ability from his earliest childhood. He composed over 600 works, including symphonies, operas, and chamber music.

Discography

Some of Mozart's most notable works include:

  • Symphony No. 40 in G minor, K. 550
  • The Magic Flute, K. 620
  • Requiem Mass in D minor, K. 626
  • Piano Concerto No. 21 in C major, K. 467
  • Don Giovanni, K. 527

Influence

Mozart's music has had a profound influence on Western music. His works are frequently performed and studied, and he is often regarded as one of the greatest composers in history.

Fun Fact

Mozart was a child prodigy, composing his first piece of music at the age of five. He performed before European royalty and was recognized as a musical genius from a young age.

Legacy

Mozart's influence extends beyond classical music; his works are frequently performed and recorded, and he is often regarded as one of the greatest composers in Western music history.
